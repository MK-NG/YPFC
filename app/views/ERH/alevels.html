A Levels

{# entry reqs #}

Advanced level qualifications (known as A levels) are subject based qualifications that can lead to university, further study, training or work.

A levels are for those aged 16 or over and are assessed by exams and coursework. You can study 3 or more A levels over 2 years.

A levels can usually be taken in school or college.

What do you need to take A levels?

You normally need:

  • at least 5 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4
  • at least grade 6 in the specific subjects you want to study
{# what they are used for #}

What can A levels be used for

You can use A level qualifications towards:

  • going to university
  • higher and degree apprenticeships
  • getting a job
{# choosing #}

Choosing A level subjects

When deciding which subjects to take at A level you should consider:

  • which subjects you are good at or would be interested in
  • which subjects or grades you might need for a certain job or further study after A levels

Find out more about A levels at: UCAS Information about A levels
