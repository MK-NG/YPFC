There are lots of work and study choices available to you. You could:

  • Combine work and study
  • Get straight into work
    • Continue your studies

    Already have a career in mind? Use our Explore Careers service.

    Combine work and study

    Combine work and study to gain skills and experience in a specific job. Explore the available options below:

    {# Vocational Technical Qualifications (VTQs) CARD #}

    Vocational Technical Qualifications (VTQs)

    Know exactly what your dream job is, but not sure how to get started? A VTQ gives the opportunity for those aged 16 to 19 years old to gain skills and experience with a work based qualification in a wide range of career options.

    {# # T LEVELS CARD #}

    T Levels

    Are you aged 16 to 19 and ready to take the next step for your future? T-levels gives you the opportunity to get real work experience with a 45-day industry placement whilst gaining a qualification that employers look for.

    {# APPRENTICESHIP CARD #}

    Apprenticeship

    Looking for the next big step in your career? Apprenticeships are industry recognised qualifications for those aged 16 and over, which will give you the opportunity to get hands-on experience by working whilst you study.

    {# TRAINEESHIP CARD #}

    Traineeship

    Upgrade your future by taking on a traineeship. If you’re aged 16 to 24 years old, this course can give you the opportunity to gain the skills and work experience you need to move into an apprenticeship.

    {# SCHOOL LEAVERS SCHEMES #}

    School Leavers Schemes

    Aimed at those who have completed A levels, work at a large company and earn money whilst you train.

    Continue your studies

    Open up a range of opportunities for your future by going into further education. Explore the available courses below:

    {# HIGHER EDUCATION CARD #}

    Higher education

    For those aged 18 and over, continue your higher education with a degree at university and gain the knowledge to find employment in a particular industry or job role.

    {# A-LEVELS CARD #}

    A Levels

    Open up a range of opportunities for your future by studying A levels. For those aged 16 and over, this subject based qualification could lead you to university, further study, training or starting work.

    {# 1 YEAR FUNDED COURSE CARD #}

    1 year funded course

    For those aged 18 to 19, take a free course to gain work related or technical skills needed by employers.



    {# HTQs) CARD #}

    Higher technical skills courses (HTQs)

    HTQs have been designed to provide you with the knowledge and skills that employers say they need. Having a HTQ can lead to a high-skilled, highly paid job in a wide range of sectors, giving you a step up in your chosen career, or further study.

    Get straight into work

    Are you ready to get into the world of work but not sure where to start? Explore the available work schemes below:

    {# KICKSTART CARD #}

    Kickstart job

    With a Kickstart job you can gain 6 months work experience whilst being paid. If you’re aged 16 to 24 and claiming universal credit, this scheme offers you the opportunity to gain the skills employers need to help you find your next job.

    {# SUPPORTED INTERNSHIP CARD #}

    Supported internship

    Are you ready to get into the world of work but not sure where to start? If you’re aged 16 to 24 with an education, health and care plan (EHC), a supported internship can give you the support you need to make the move into employment.

    {# INTERNSHIPS AND VOLUNTEERING CARD #}

    Internships and volunteering

    Gain work experience in a chosen job and learn about the industry by getting an internship or volunteering.

    {# GET A JOB #}

    Get a job

    Get straight into the working world by getting a job to start earning and gaining experience.

    {# START A BUSINESS CARD #}

    Start a business

    If you have a great business idea or want to be an entrepreneur, starting your own business might be right for you.
