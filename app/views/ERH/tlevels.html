T Levels

T Levels are a qualification for students aged 16 to 19 who’ve finished their GCSEs. They are a 2-year qualification and are equivalent to 3 A levels.

You’ll spend 80% of your time in the classroom and 20% on a minimum 9-week placement with an employer, meaning you can gain industry experience while you continue your studies.

T Levels can help you into skilled employment, higher study or apprenticeships.

How much does a T level cost

Like A levels, there are no tuition fees to study a T Level if you start before you are 19.

What does a T Level include

A T Level course will allow you to gain a technical qualification and learn specialist skills and knowledge for your chosen occupation or career.

T Levels include an industry placement which will last for at least 9 weeks (or 45 days) – when and how you complete it depends on the T Level, college and employer.

During your placement you will:

  • have a line manager who will supervise and support you
  • be provided with feedback at the end of your placement

What can I study?

Here are the T Level subjects currently available, with more to be added from September 2022 onwards:

  • Design, Surveying and Planning for Construction
  • Digital Business Services
  • Digital Production, Design and Development

Read about the upcoming T Level subjects.

How to apply for a T Level

There are 2 steps to applying for a T level.

  • Search for a T Level (opens in a new tab)
  • Contact the training provider against offering the most suitable one.
