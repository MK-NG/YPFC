A Levels

This subject based qualification, for those aged 16 and over, can lead to university, further study, training, or work.

{# entry reqs #}

A levels are subject-based qualifications assessed by exams. They can lead to further study, training or work.

You usually study between 1 and 5 A levels over 2 years. Most students choose 3 subjects to study but you can take more or fewer. You can even study for a BTEC or other qualification at the same time.

Grades you need to take A levels

You normally need:

  • At least 5 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4
  • At least grade 6 in the subjects you want to study
{# choosing #}

Choosing your A level subjects

When deciding which subjects to take at A level, you should consider:

  • which subjects you're good at or would be interested in
  • wha grades you might need for a certain job or further study after A levels

Find out more about A levels at: UCAS Information about A levels
