Higher education

For those aged 18 and over, continue your higher education with a degree at university and gain the knowledge to find employment in a particular industry or job role.

You may wish to continue your studies with a higher education qualification.

If you want to move on to a higher level of learning, you could study at a university, college, Institute of Technology (IoT) or a National College. There are opportunities to learn online as well as to study part-time.

Higher technical skills courses

Learn the higher-level skills that employers want in sectors like finance, engineering, management and science.

HTQs are usually taught at a college or university and take 1 to 2 years to complete.

More about HTQs

University degree

An academic course that's usually studied at a higher education institution.

Learn through lectures, seminars, group projects and independent study. A degree course takes 3 to 4 years to complete.

There's funding available to support you and you can study full or part-time.

Entry requirements for a degree

Universities set their own entry requirements for degrees. You'll usually need A levels or equivalent qualifications.

If you do not have the qualifications you need for a degree, you can take an access to higher education diploma.

Options after university

You can use your degree towards:

  • postgraduate degrees
  • professional qualifications
  • graduate employment programmes
  • getting a job

Find out more about undergraduate degrees on UCAS
