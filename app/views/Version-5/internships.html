Internships and volunteering

Gain work experience in a chosen job and learn about the industry.

Volunteering

If you don’t have much work experience to make your CV stand out, you can gain experience and develop your skills through volunteering.

Through volunteering you can:

  • find out what's involved in a job to see if it's for you
  • learn new skills or build on ones you already have
  • meet new people and develop your network of contacts
  • boost your confidence
  • give back to your community

How to choose a volunteering position

You may have to take small steps to get to your perfect volunteering opportunity. It can be very competitive and for some volunteering opportunities you might need to be over 18.

Research the sector you’re interested in and look for roles where you could develop your soft skills, such as:

  • public speaking
  • organisation
  • leadership
  • communication

Internship

An internship can give you the opportunity to gain skills and experience in a place of work or a particular industry.

The length of the internship is decided by the business but they are usually a few weeks or months long and can be done around work or study.

An internship might include:

  • shadowing members of the business to learn what the day-to-day tasks of the job include
  • a training programme to help the assess your skills

An internship can help you to understand if the company or industry is right for you.
