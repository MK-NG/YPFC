Supported internship

For those aged 16 to 24, these internships provide extra support for those with an education, health and care plan (EHC) to move into employment.

A supported internship is a work-based study programme for 16 to 24-year-olds with special educational needs.

They usually last for one year and help prepare you for paid employment through learning in the workplace.

Starting a supported internship

To start a supported internship, you’ll need to:

  • be aged 16 to 24
  • have an education, health and care plan (EHC)

You can apply for a supported internship while you’re still at school but you’ll need to be 16 or over by the end of the summer holidays to start the internship.

What you’ll do as a supported intern

On a supported internship, you’ll spend around 70% of your learning time in the workplace. Alongside your time with the employer, you’ll enrol with a learning provider.

A supported internship includes:

  • an unpaid work placement with support from a qualified job coach
  • a personalised study programme delivered by the learning provider

You’ll also have the chance to study a relevant substantial qualification, if suitable.This is in addition to studying English and maths to an appropriate level.

Applying for a supportred internship

You can apply for supported internships through your:

  • school or college
  • social worker
  • transition worker
  • local Jobcentre Plus

Help and advice

You can get help and advice from:

  • Preparing for Adulthood - they have a factsheet on supported internships and guidance on study programmes for learners with learning difficulties and disabilities
  • Talking Futures - support for parents and guardians looking to explore education and career options with their child
  • CEC Resource Directory - support for teachers
