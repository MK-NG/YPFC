Supported internship

For those aged 16 to 24, these internships provide extra support for those with an education, health and care plan (EHC) to move into employment.

{# cost #}

How much do supported internships cost

Supported internships are free. The employer and training provider determine their costs together with government support.

If you’re under 25 when you start your apprenticeship and have previously been in local authority care, you may be eligible for a bursary payment.

{# who #}

Who can apply for supported internships

To start supported internships, you’ll need to be:

  • 16 or over
  • living in England
  • not in full-time education

You can apply for supported internships while you’re still at school but you’ll need to be 16 or over by the end of the summer holidays to start the internship.

{# include #}

What does a supported internship include

As an apprentice you will:

  • be an employee earning a wage and getting holiday pay
  • work alongside experienced staff
  • gain job-specific skills
  • get time for training and study related to your role

As an intern, at least 20% of your normal working hours must be spent on training. Your training might happen every week, every month or in a separate block of time.

The training might take place:

  • at your place of work
  • somewhere else like at a college or training provider
  • online
{# pay #}

Pay and working hours

There are different rates of pay for apprentices depending on your age and what year of your apprenticeship you’re in.

Your employment contract should confirm your rate of pay but you must be paid the appropriate national minimum wage (opens in a new tab).

You must be paid for:

  • your normal working hours (found in your employment contract or apprenticeship agreement)
  • training that’s part of your apprenticeship (at least 20% of your normal working hours)
  • study towards English and maths qualifications, if they’re part of your apprenticeship

There are rules about how many hours you can work in a week and being paid overtime.

You’ll get at least 20 days of paid holiday per year, plus bank holidays. The holiday calculator (opens in a new tab) helps you check what you are allowed to take.

{# how #}

How to apply for A-Levels

There are 3 steps to applying for A-Levels.

  • Search for A-Levels (opens in a new tab)
  • Sign in or create an account.
  • Complete and submit your application.
{# unsuccessful #}

What to do if your apprenticeship application is unsuccessful

You can ask for feedback if you do not get selected for an interview or for the apprenticeship.

You can complain if you think you were not successful because you were discriminated against, or your treatment in the interview or application process was unfair.

Contact Acas for free and confidential advice on your rights at work. Contact the Support Service for Apprentices for mental health support and advice.

{# help #}

Get help and advice

Your school, college or training provider may be able to give you advice about apprenticeships.

The National Careers Service (opens in a new tab) has advice on writing applications and what to do at interviews.

You can call the Apprenticeship helpline if you were made redundant from your last apprenticeship and you can also talk to your training provider.
