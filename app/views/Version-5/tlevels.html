T Levels

For those aged 18 and over, continue your higher education with a degree at university and gain the knowledge to find employment in a particular industry or job role.

Najeeb's story

“I’ve learnt a lot from the quantity surveying team, the site management team, even the health and safety team, the design team. I’ve learnt from all of them because I’ve had a taste of all their roles.”

A T Level is a vocational technical qualification for 16 to 19-year-olds that lasts for 2 years. 1 T Level is the equivalent of 3 A levels.

You’ll gain a qualification and learn specialist skills for your chosen career through a mix of classroom learning and an industry placement.

What you’ll do on a T Level course

You’ll spend 80% of your time in the classroom and 20% on a minimum 9-week placement with an employer. This means you can gain industry experience while you continue your studies.

In the classroom you will learn the core knowledge, concepts and theories relevant to your chosen industry, and choose a specialism that will give you the knowledge and skills you need to get a good job in a specific area.

When and how you complete ithe industry placementt depends on the T Level, college and employer.

During your placement you'll:

  • have a line manager who will supervise and support you
  • be provided with feedback at the end of your placement

Options after a T Level

T Levels can lead directly into work or on to further study such as a related higher level apprenticeship or a degree course.

Here are some of the careers that a T Level can lead to:

  • Laboratory technician
  • Computer games developer
  • Dental nurse
  • Electrical engineering technician
  • Healthcare science assistant
  • Teaching assistant
