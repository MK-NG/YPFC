Traineeship

A course for 16 to 24-year-olds that includes work experience to help you get ready for work or an apprenticeship.

Traineeships help 16 to 24-year-olds get ready for an apprenticeship or job if they do not have the right skills or experience.

They include a work placement and can last from 6 weeks up to 1 year, though most traineeships last for less than 6 months.

Traineeships are free

Who can apply for a traineeship

To start a traineeship, you'll need to be:

  • eligible to work in England
  • unemployed and have little or no work experience
  • aged 16 to 24 and qualified up to and including Level 3 (equivalent to A-Level)

What does a traineeship include

Traineeships can be tailored to meet your needs and prepare you for what local businesses are looking for.

From the training provider you get:

  • training to prepare you for work, including CV writing and what to expect in the workplace
  • support to improve your English, maths and digital skills (if you need it)

From the employer you get:

  • a high-quality work placement of at least 70 hours
  • an interview for an apprenticeship or job (if they have a vacancy), or written feedback

Pay and working hours

Traineeships are not jobs and employers are not required to pay you for the work placement, but they can support you with expenses such as transport and meals.

If you are eligible, you can maintain your entitlement to benefits whilst on a traineeship.

You may get:

  • expenses for transport and meals from your employer
  • financial support for travel, childcare or a disability from your training provider
  • support from your local Jobcentre Plus if you are eligible for work benefits

How to find a traineeship

You can use our find a traineeship service (opens in a new tab) or by speaking to your:

  • local college or training provider
  • school careers advisers if you’re 16 to 18 years old
  • Jobcentre Plus adviser if you’re in receipt of benefits
