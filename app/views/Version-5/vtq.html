Vocational technical qualifications (VTQs)

For those aged 18 and over, continue your higher education with a degree at university and gain the knowledge to find employment in a particular industry or job role.

VTQs are practical qualifications for over 16s about a specific job or career sector.

They are designed to help you get the skills you need to start a job, progress in a career, or go on to higher levels of education.

VTQs are usually taken at a college.

{# who #}

Starting a VTQ

To start a VTQ, you’ll need to:

  • be 16 or over
  • live in England
  • not be in full-time education

You can apply for VTQs while you’re still at school but you’ll need to be 16 or over by the end of the summer holidays to start the VTQ.

{# include #}

Types of VTQs

T Levels, BTECs and OCR nationals are all types of VTQs.

VTQs can be taken in vocational subjects and courses that can lead to a:

  • broad employment area, such as business, engineering or IT
  • specific job, such as hairdresser, accountant or plumber

The length of the VTQ will be different for each one, but the minimum length is one year.

{# include #}

Entry requirements

You normally need:

  • at least 5 GCSEs at grades 3 to 1
  • a level 1 qualification
