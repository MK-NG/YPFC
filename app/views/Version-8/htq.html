Higher technical skills courses (HTQs)

For those aged 18 and over, this classroom-based technical training qualification can give you a step up in your chosen career or further study. Providing you with the knowledge and skills to get a job.

If you’re aged 18 and over, this classroom-based technical training qualification can provide you with the knowledge and skills that employers need.

Having a HTQ can lead to a high-skilled, highly paid job in different sectors, giving you a step up in your chosen career, or further study.

Tuition fees

Tuition fees vary depending on the course. They’re likely to cost from around £7,000 to a maximum of £9,250 per year for a 1 or 2 year course.

Level of qualification

HTQs are Level 4 and 5 qualifications, which sit between A Levels, T Levels, and degrees. They’re approved by the Institute for Apprenticeships and Technical Education.

Entry requirements

Each educational provider sets their own entry requirements for HTQs.

Starting a HTQ

To start a HTQ, you’ll need to:

  • be aged 18 or over
  • live in England
  • not be in full-time education

You can apply for a HTQ while you’re still at school, but you’ll need to be 18 or over by the end of the summer holidays to start the course.

Work experience

There is no mandatory work placement included on a HTQ. However many do provide an experience of the workplace through workplace simulations, virtual workplaces, and work experience.

Options after a HTQ

After completing a HTQ, you can go on to further study or enter your chosen career knowing you have the right skills to succeed.

HTQ subjects

The first HTQs will be taught from September 2022 and will be tailored to the digital sector. More sectors will be rolled out over a four-year period, including Health, Science and Construction in 2023.

Occupations a Digital HTQ could lead to include:

  • Network Engineer
  • Software Developer
  • Data Analyst

You can apply directly to a provider or submit an application through UCAS.
